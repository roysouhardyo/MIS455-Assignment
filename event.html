EEE Innovation Fest 2024 unites industry and academia

The EEE Innovation Fest 2024, hosted by the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) of Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB), took place on October 16, 2024 at the university campus. The event brought together students and academics from more than 10 departments across five schools of IUB, along with industry professionals and tech enthusiasts, to showcase cutting-edge innovations and research in electrical and electronic engineering and other disciplines. Organized by the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society IUB Student Branch Chapter, the IEEE Industry Application Society IUB Student Branch Chapter, and the IEEE IUB Student Branch, the event featured project exhibitions, tech competitions, and student presentations.

IUB Assistant Coach Monika becomes Runner-up in Ispahani Squash Championship 2024

Marjan Akter Monika, Assistant Squash Coach at Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB), has clinched the Runner-up position in the Ispahani 4th Bangladesh Squash Championship PSA Tournament 2024. In the tournament, held from October 1 to 5, 2024, at the Army Complex in Dhaka, Monika delivered a strong performance, competing against top players from Kuwait, Iran, Malaysia, India, Egypt, Sri Lanka, and Bangladesh in the Women’s Open category. She advanced through multiple rounds, defeating several international and local opponents before securing her place in the final match. She faced Sri Lanka’s Yeheni Kuruppu in the final, where she suffered a 3-0 defeat. Champion Kuruppu received a prize money of $190, while Runner-up Monika was awarded $120.

30 climate enthusiasts participate in IUB’s short course on LLA and L&D

The International Centre for Climate Change and Development (ICCCAD), the Outreach, Extension and Continuing Education (OECE) unit of the Office of the Pro-Vice Chancellor and the Department of Environmental Science and Management (DESM) at Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB), organized the second cohort of a short course titled “Locally Led Adaptation and Local Loss and Damage: A Nexus Approach.” The four-day program, held from September 30 to October 3, 2024, brought together 30 participants from academic and professional backgrounds across Bangladesh. Participants, including lecturers, researchers, and professionals from institutions such as BRAC, the University of Dhaka, and Jashore University of Science and Technology, engaged in six sessions exploring key aspects of Locally Led Adaptation (LLA) in the context of climate change. The course covered the evolution of LLA from Community-based Adaptation (CBA), discussed its core principles, and addressed the practical applications of LLA in mitigating both economic and non-economic losses. Special focus was placed on Nature-based Solutions (NbS) and the challenges of scaling LLA globally.
